Hastinapur

History

The Hastinapur is describe in the Mahabharata, Hastinapur is portrayed as the capital of the Kuru Kingdom of the Kauravas. Many incidents in the Mahabharata were set in the city of Hastinapur. Hastinapur is described in Hindu texts such as the Mahabharata and the Puranas as the capital of the Kuru Kingdom. It is also mentioned in ancient Jain texts. As per Jain texts, Hastinapur was one of the Mahajanpads established by Bhagwan Adinath, who is also known as Rishab Dev.

Interesting Facts for tourists to visit

  • Pandeshwar temple is a famous temple in Hastinapur. It is dedicated to Shiva and is believed to be the place where Kauravas and Pandavas received their education in Vedas and Puranas.
  • Other temples in Hastinapur include Jambudweep Jain Tirth, Digamber Jain Bada Mandir, Kailash Parvat, and Karna Temple.
  • Pandav Temple is also a place of interest in Hastinapur.

Time to Visit
